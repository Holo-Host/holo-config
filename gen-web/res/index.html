A few things to note before you get started:

What's next?

  • Power on and connect your HoloPort to the Internet
  • Allow your HoloPort to update. When the light on your HoloPort stops flashing green, the update process is complete
  • Have the USB drive included with your HoloPort at hand
  • Be sure to register your HoloPort using the same email you purchased it with
  • Keep a copy of the password you create for registration somewhere secure--cryptographic keys are not replaceable
  • Key Generation: The email and password you enter will be used to generate your cryptographic keys locally in your browser. They will be temporarily stored in your browser’s memory.
  • Fun Fact: Cryptographic keys are like digital keys that can be used to lock and unlock your data in a highly secure way.

  • Thanks for registreing your HoloPort!

  • You'll receive an email that includes a link to your Host Console where you can login and manage the settings of your HoloPort.

Step 1: Registration Code

Enter the registration code you received in your email.

Step 2: Generate a master seed

Enter a passphrase. This is the most important thing to remember since it decrypts your master seed, keep it somewhere very safe. You can turn off the internet while doing this for added security.
Tip: To ensure your passphrase is as strong as possible, make it long, include a mix of many different types of characters, and make it hard to guess.

Step 3: Create Host Console credentials

Enter the email address you used to register as a Host, and create a Host Console password for your primary HoloPort

Step 4: Create your keys and save them

Save the configuration file to the USB drive associated with this HoloPort. The filename must be for your HoloPort to read it.

Step 5: Connect to the Holo Network

Safely eject the USB from your computer and insert it into the associated HoloPort.
